Reading the overlay: line direction arrows show the direction of flight. Enroute transitions draw thin, the common route thick, and runway transitions / finals medium — select a whole STAR to watch its enroute feeds funnel into the common route and fan back out to each runway. Altitude windows at a fix read like the chart: ≥ at-or-above, ≤ at-or-below, a range is a window, @ mandatory. Dashed grey legs are heading/vector segments (drawn to an estimated length); dashed red is the missed approach. In the 3D view the ribbon height is altitude (vertical scale exaggerated; posts drop to the ground track). Data: FAA CIFP, for situational awareness only — not for navigation.
