The invisible architecture of controlled and uncontrolled air, and who talks to whom.

- Airspace Classes {#airspace-classes} :: A B C D E and G — each with its own entry, equipment, weather and clearance rules.
  - Class B / C / D :: Terminal airspace around busy airports. B needs a clearance and Mode C/ADS-B; C/D need two-way radio contact established before entry. -> airworthiness "equipment required"
  - Class E & G :: E is controlled airspace without a tower; G is uncontrolled. The dividing floors (700/1200 AGL) set which VFR minimums apply. -> vfr-weather-minimums "sets VFR minimums"
  - Special Use & TFRs :: Prohibited, restricted, MOA, alert and warning areas — plus temporary flight restrictions. Check NOTAMs; the DC SFRA/FRZ is home turf near KANP.
- VFR Weather Minimums {#vfr-weather-minimums} :: The "3-152" cloud-clearance and visibility rules that keep VFR traffic able to see and avoid — they tighten as the airspace gets busier.
- ATC Services :: Clearances, separation and advisories — the human layer.
  - Clearances & Readbacks :: An ATC clearance is an authorization, not an instruction to violate safety. Read back anything that keeps you clear of others or the ground.
  - Flight Following {#flight-following} :: VFR radar advisories — traffic calls and a controller watching, workload permitting. Cheap insurance.
  - Radio Phraseology :: Who you are, where you are, what you want. Standard phraseology keeps a shared frequency unambiguous. -> communication "communication"
- Airport Operations :: The rules and rhythm of the field itself.
  - Traffic Pattern {#traffic-pattern} :: Standard left turns unless charted otherwise: upwind, crosswind, downwind, base, final. At KANP both runways are left traffic — don't infer the side from geometry. -> takeoffs-and-landings "sets up the landing"
  - Markings, Signs & Lighting :: Runway/taxiway paint, hold-short lines, mandatory (red) and location (black) signs, VASI/PAPI glidepath lights.
  - Non-Towered Ops :: Self-announce on CTAF, standard pattern, see-and-avoid — the KANP everyday. -> communication "CTAF calls"
