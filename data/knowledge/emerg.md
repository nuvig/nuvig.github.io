When the plan breaks — the flows, the priorities and the decisions rehearsed cold.

- Engine & Power Loss {#engine-and-power-loss} :: The prop stops or falters — fly the airplane first, then work the problem on the way down.
  - Complete Power Loss :: Pitch for best glide, pick a field, run the flow — fuel selector, mixture rich, boost pump, mags both, primer in, carb heat — then Mayday/7700, secure, and land. Aviate the whole way.
  - Best Glide & Field Selection :: Trim to L/Dmax for maximum range, judge wind and slope, commit to a field early and fly a normal-looking high-key/low-key pattern to it. -> emergency-airwork "L/Dmax"
  - Partial Power & Rough Running :: Diagnose fuel, air (carb ice), spark and mixture. Carb heat and a mixture sweep cure a surprising share of roughness — nurse it toward an airport, never trust it.
  - Engine Failure After Takeoff :: Low and slow with few options: lower the nose NOW to hold glide speed and land within a shallow arc ahead. The turn-back "impossible turn" stalls and spins the unwary — pick an altitude gate in advance. -> stall "avoid the stall/spin"
  - Forced & Precautionary Landing :: Forced = must land now; precautionary = choosing to land while you still can (weather, fuel, lost). Brief passengers, tighten belts, fuel/mags off short final, minimum energy at touchdown.
- Fire & Smoke :: The one emergency where speed beats troubleshooting — cut the source, ventilate deliberately, and land as soon as possible.
  - Engine Fire :: Mixture idle-cutoff, fuel selector off, cabin heat/air off, then a forced landing — do not attempt a restart. A sideslip can keep flames off the cabin.
  - Electrical Fire :: Acrid smoke and a hot smell: master/battery off, kill the bus, land. If systems are essential, add items back one at a time to find the culprit. -> electrical-failure "electrical system"
  - Cabin Fire :: Extinguisher (PASS), then ventilate — a fire fed by fresh air may mean closing vents first. Descend and land immediately; smoke is as dangerous as flame.
  - Carbon Monoxide {#carbon-monoxide} :: Odorless exhaust from a cracked heater shroud — headache, sleepiness, then incapacitation. Heater off, fresh-air vents open, oxygen if available, and land.
- System & Instrument Failures :: Something quits — recognize the pattern, work around it, and land where help is.
  - Electrical Failure {#electrical-failure} :: Alternator warning / discharge: reduce load, run the checklist, and treat the battery as a countdown to essential-only. Know exactly what you lose when it goes dark.
  - Vacuum / Partial Panel {#vacuum-partial-panel} :: Gyros die slowly and lie convincingly — the classic killer. Cover them, fly the electric turn coordinator + ASI + altimeter, and get to VFR. -> gyroscopic-instruments "lost gyros"
  - Pitot-Static Failures :: A blocked pitot or static port gives a predictable but deadly-wrong reading — cross-check attitude + power, select the alternate static source, and trust the picture that agrees. -> pitot-static-instruments "the instruments"
  - Gear & Flap Malfunction :: Gear that won't extend (manual/emergency extension, positive-g yaw), or asymmetric/split flaps — fly the airplane, run the checklist, and plan a longer, flatter arrival.
  - Trim & Autopilot Runaway :: A runaway trim or misbehaving autopilot: disconnect it (autopilot switch, then its breaker), hold the airplane with muscle, and re-trim by hand.
- Loss of Control & Upset :: The airplane departs controlled flight or points somewhere alarming — recover with the wings first.
  - Stall / Spin Recovery :: Stall: reduce AoA (nose down), full power, level the wings, minimum altitude loss. Spin: PARE — Power idle, Ailerons neutral, Rudder opposite the spin, Elevator forward. -> stall "the aerodynamics"
  - Unusual Attitude Recovery :: Nose-high near stall: add power, unload, level the wings. Nose-low overspeed: reduce power, level the wings, ease out. Bank before pitch, and believe the instruments. -> v-speeds-and-the-envelope "respect the envelope"
  - Spiral Dive :: Airspeed and bank increasing, altitude unwinding: power idle, roll the wings level FIRST, then gently raise the nose — pulling before leveling only tightens it.
  - Inadvertent VFR-into-IMC :: The deadliest trap: commit to the instruments, a standard-rate 180° turn back toward VMC, resist the leans, and ask for help early. -> spatial-disorientation "disorientation" -> aeronautical-decision-making "the decision chain"
- Weather & Environmental :: Getting out of conditions the airplane can't survive in.
  - Inadvertent Icing Encounter :: Without known-ice gear: pitot heat on, exit the conditions — climb to warmer air or descend below the freezing level, add power and speed, and carry extra on approach; never let it stall. -> airframe-icing "airframe ice"
  - Storm / Severe Turbulence :: If trapped: slow to maneuvering speed, hold attitude (not altitude), lights up, avoid abrupt inputs, and turn to the shortest way out. -> thunderstorms "the storm" -> v-speeds-and-the-envelope "fly Va"
  - Wind Shear / Microburst Escape :: On approach: full power, pitch to the attitude/flight-director for maximum climb, don't chase airspeed, and go around early. Never try to fly through a microburst. -> wind-shear-and-microburst "the shear"
  - Lost & Disoriented :: The five Cs: Climb (for radio/radar/nav range), Communicate, Confess, Comply, Conserve. Use flight following, DF steers and every nav aid before fuel decides for you. -> communication "confess & ask"
- Cabin & Passengers :: The emergencies that happen behind the panel.
  - Door or Window Open :: Startling noise, rarely a real handling problem — fly the airplane, don't grab for it in a critical phase, and land to secure it. Most cabin doors can't be closed in flight.
  - Passenger Incapacitation :: Brief passengers before flight; if someone is ill or panicking, delegate tasks, use medical help relayed via ATC, and land at the nearest suitable airport.
  - Hypoxia / Depressurization :: Insidious oxygen starvation: don oxygen, descend to a safe altitude immediately, and treat any unexplained euphoria or fumbling as hypoxia until proven otherwise. -> hypoxia "the physiology"
- Emergency Comms & Equipment :: Getting help, and the gear that summons it.
  - Distress vs Urgency :: MAYDAY×3 = distress (grave, immediate danger); PAN-PAN×3 = urgency (a problem, not yet dire). State who, what, where, intentions, and souls/fuel. -> communication "the comm layer"
  - Squawk & 121.5 :: Transponder 7700 (general emergency), 7600 (lost comms), 7500 (hijack); 121.5 is the guard frequency monitored everywhere. ATC can help even before you've reached them.
  - ATC Assistance & DF Steer :: ATC can give vectors, no-gyro steers, weather avoidance, light-gun signals and priority handling — declaring an emergency unlocks all of it. Ask, and accept the help.
  - ELT & Survival :: 406 MHz ELTs alert satellites with a GPS position; know how to trigger it manually, and carry what you'd need to be found and to wait for rescue.
- Priorities & Checklists :: The discipline that makes all of the above survivable.
  - Aviate, Navigate, Communicate :: In that order, always. Fly the airplane first; a radio call or a checklist that steals attention from flying is how a survivable problem becomes a crash.
  - Memory Items vs Read-Do :: A handful of immediate actions are done from memory (engine fire, stall); everything else is read-and-do off the checklist once the airplane is stable and time allows.
  - Decision-Making Under Pressure :: Buy time (climb, hold, circle), name the problem, weigh the options, decide — then commit. Startle and tunnel vision are the real enemies. -> aeronautical-decision-making "ADM under load"
