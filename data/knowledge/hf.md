The pilot as the least predictable system — decision-making, physiology and illusions.

- Aeronautical Decision Making {#aeronautical-decision-making} :: A systematic approach to the mental side of flying: recognize hazards, weigh risk, decide, act, and evaluate — before the airplane decides for you.
  - Hazardous Attitudes :: Anti-authority, impulsivity, invulnerability, macho, resignation — each with a spoken antidote. Naming yours is half the cure.
  - Risk Management (PAVE / 5P) :: Pilot, Aircraft, enVironment, External pressures — and the 5P checkpoints (Plan, Plane, Pilot, Passengers, Programming) revisited in flight.
  - IMSAFE & Personal Minimums :: Illness, Medication, Stress, Alcohol, Fatigue, Eating/Emotion — a self-check against limits you set on the ground, not in the air.
- Aeromedical Factors :: How altitude and stress act on the body.
  - Hypoxia {#hypoxia} :: Oxygen starvation — insidious, euphoric, deadly. Regulatory oxygen above 12,500 ft (after 30 min) and always above 14,000 ft.
    - Four Types :: Hypoxic (low ambient O₂ / altitude), hypemic (blood can't carry it — CO, anemia), stagnant (blood not moving — g, cold), histotoxic (cells can't use it — alcohol). -> carbon-monoxide "hypemic = CO"
    - Symptoms & TUC :: Euphoria, poor judgment, tunnel vision, cyanosis — and no alarm from the pilot. Time of useful consciousness collapses with altitude: minutes at 18,000, seconds above 30,000.
    - Supplemental Oxygen (91.211) :: Crew O₂ above 12,500 ft after 30 min and continuously above 14,000 ft; passengers offered above 15,000 ft. Cabin altitude, not aircraft altitude, is what counts. -> pressure-altitude "cabin altitude"
  - Hyperventilation :: Over-breathing from stress blows off CO₂ — tingling, dizziness, mimics hypoxia. Slow the breathing, talk, breathe into a bag.
  - Spatial Disorientation {#spatial-disorientation} :: The inner ear lies without outside references — the vestibular and visual systems disagree and the pilot loses the horizon. -> gyroscopic-instruments "why you trust the gyros"
    - The Leans :: A slow roll goes unfelt, so rolling level feels like a bank the other way. The most common illusion — believe the attitude indicator, not the seat.
    - Graveyard Spiral :: In a prolonged turn the sensation of turning fades; correcting the resulting altitude loss by pulling only tightens the descending spiral.
    - Somatogravic Illusion :: Forward acceleration (a go-around, takeoff into a black hole) feels like a pitch-up, tempting a dangerous push-over. Deceleration feels like a dive.
    - Coriolis Illusion :: Moving your head during a turn tumbles the fluid in all three canals at once — an overwhelming false rotation. Keep head movements slow in IMC.
  - Carbon Monoxide :: Odorless exhaust gas from a cracked heater shroud — headache, sleepiness. Cabin heat off, vents open, land. -> carbon-monoxide "from the heater"
  - Fatigue & IMSAFE Physiology :: Acute vs chronic fatigue erodes judgment and reaction like alcohol; add hypoglycemia, dehydration and stress. The body's limits belong on the ground checklist.
  - Vision & Night :: Rods for night/peripheral, off-center viewing, 30-min dark adaptation, and the black-hole approach illusion.
- Optical Illusions on Landing :: Runway width, up/down-sloping terrain, and featureless approaches distort your perceived glidepath — cross-check the VASI/PAPI and the numbers. -> takeoffs-and-landings "bias the flare"
- Communication & CRM :: Single-pilot resource management: use every resource — ATC, checklists, automation, passengers — and communicate clearly. -> communication "clear comms"
- Communication {#communication} :: Clear, standard, timely radio and crew communication — the thread that ties ATC, CRM and emergencies together.
