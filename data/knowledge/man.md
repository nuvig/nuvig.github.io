The stick-and-rudder skills, from the first takeoff to the checkride tasks.

- Basic Airwork :: The building blocks flown to ACS tolerances.
  - Straight & Level / Trim :: Hold heading and altitude by attitude + power, trimming off the pressure. The reference for everything else.
  - Climbs, Descents & Turns :: Coordinated entries and rollouts, pitch for speed, power for rate, rudder to keep the ball centered.
  - Slow Flight & Stalls {#slow-flight-and-stalls} :: Flight at high AoA near the stall, and power-on/power-off stall recognition and recovery — the feel of the wing running out of margin.
  - Steep Turns :: 45–50° banked level turns holding altitude — a lesson in load factor, back-pressure and coordinated rudder. -> v-speeds-and-the-envelope "loads the wing" -> load-factor "2 g at 60°" -> maneuvering-speed-va "stay below Va"
- Ground Reference :: Flying precise tracks over the ground while the wind tries to blow you off them.
  - Turns Around a Point / S-Turns :: Vary bank to hold a constant-radius track — steepest downwind, shallowest upwind. Pure wind-drift correction. -> wind-correction-triangle "wind drift"
  - Eights on Pylons :: Hold the wingtip pinned on a pylon by flying at pivotal altitude (≈GS²/11.3) — elevator, not aileron, chases the pylon. (See the interactive explainer.) -> groundspeed-and-ld "uses groundspeed"
- Takeoffs & Landings {#takeoffs-and-landings} :: Normal, crosswind, short-field and soft-field variants — the parts of every flight most likely to bite.
  - Crosswind Technique :: Wing-low slip or crab-to-kick: aileron into the wind, opposite rudder to align with the runway. Limited by control authority and the demonstrated crosswind. -> wind-shear-and-microburst "gusts / shear"
  - Short & Soft Field :: Short-field: max performance over an obstacle at Vx. Soft-field: weight off the wheels early, in ground effect, never stopping on soft ground. -> ground-effect "uses ground effect" -> region-of-reversed-command "flown behind the curve"
  - Stabilized Approach & Go-Around :: On speed, on glidepath, configured and trimmed by a gate altitude — or go around. The decision that prevents most landing accidents. -> region-of-reversed-command "low-and-slow trap"
- Emergency Airwork {#emergency-airwork} :: Best glide, engine-out field selection and the flows that live in muscle memory. -> groundspeed-and-ld "fly L/Dmax" -> engine-and-power-loss "engine failure"
