The 14 CFR framework — who may fly, in what, when, and how currency and airworthiness are proven.

- Certificates & Ratings :: Student → private → commercial → ATP, plus instrument and category/class ratings. Each unlocks privileges and carries limits.
  - Medical Certificates :: First/second/third class, or BasicMed. The medical you need matches the privileges you exercise.
  - Currency & Recency :: Flight review every 24 mo; 3 takeoffs/landings in 90 days for passengers (night = full-stop); 6 approaches + holding + tracking in 6 mo for IFR. -> ifr-regulations-and-currency "IFR currency"
- Airworthiness {#airworthiness} :: The airplane must be in a condition for safe flight and have required inspections done. Pilot and owner share the responsibility.
  - Required Inspections :: Annual (12 mo), 100-hour if for hire, transponder (24 mo), pitot-static (24 mo for IFR), ELT (battery/12 mo), altimeter.
  - Required Equipment (ATOMATOFLAMES / FLAPS) :: Day-VFR minimum equipment by memory aid; night adds fuses, landing light, anti-collision, position lights, source of power.
  - MEL & Inoperative Equipment :: With no MEL, an inop item must be required-or-removed/placarded per 91.213 and a pilot/mechanic determination of safety.
- Right-of-Way & Ops Rules :: Who yields (91.113), the 250 KIAS-below-10,000 limit, minimum safe altitudes (91.119), and sterile-cockpit discipline.
- Preflight Action (91.103) :: All available information: weather, runway lengths, performance, alternates, fuel, NOTAMs, and for IFR an alternate plan. "NWKRAFT." -> aeronautical-decision-making "part of good ADM"
