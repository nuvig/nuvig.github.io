What the airplane can actually do today, and whether it's loaded to do it safely.

- Takeoff & Landing Performance :: Ground roll and distance over a 50-ft obstacle from the POH charts — corrected for density altitude, weight, wind, slope and surface. -> density-altitude "driven by density altitude"
- Climb Performance :: Excess thrust vs excess power: Vx (best angle, most altitude per distance — obstacle) vs Vy (best rate, most altitude per time).
  - Vx vs Vy :: Vx clears the trees; Vy gets you up fastest. They converge with altitude and meet at the absolute ceiling.
  - Service & Absolute Ceiling :: Service ceiling = 100 fpm climb remains; absolute = climb goes to zero. Both fall as weight and density altitude rise.
- Weight & Balance :: Total weight within limits AND the CG within the envelope. Moment = weight × arm; sum moments, divide by weight, find the CG.
  - Center of Gravity {#center-of-gravity} :: The balance point. Forward CG: stable, higher stall speed, more elevator needed to flare. Aft CG: less stable, lighter controls, worse spin recovery. -> load-and-stall-link "affects stall speed"
  - Loading & Envelope :: Plot the loaded point on the CG envelope; shift baggage or fuel to stay inside for the whole flight as fuel burns.
- V-Speeds & the Envelope {#v-speeds-and-the-envelope} :: The alphabet of airspeeds — Vso, Vs1, Vx, Vy, Va, Vno, Vne, Vfe — and the V-n diagram that bounds load factor against speed.
  - Maneuvering Speed (Va) {#maneuvering-speed-va} :: Below Va the wing stalls before it overstresses — full deflection is structurally safe. Va decreases as weight decreases. -> wind-shear-and-microburst "slow for turbulence"
  - Airspeed Colour Arcs :: White (flap range), green (normal), yellow (caution, smooth air only), red line (never exceed) on the ASI.
