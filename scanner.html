Gain = how hard the receiver amplifies: higher hears weaker/farther signals but distorts close ones (meter peaking red = too high). Squelch = mutes the channel until a signal is present: higher kills more hiss but starts eating quiet voices. If you can't hear a weak transmission you know is there, lower the squelch first. The bar shows SNR — how far the signal stands above the noise floor. Grey/low = nothing there; green = a solid signal. Changes apply instantly.
